Background

Who I am

I'm an ML research engineer who completed my M.Tech at DA-IICT, Gandhinagar (2026), specializing in speech representation learning and evaluation methodology for pathological speech.

My work has been shaped by a recurring problem: building systems that fail in ways standard benchmarks don't catch — and shipping the pipelines to prove it. In speaker verification for dysarthric speech, I found that cosine similarity metrics produced misleading results because pathological speech sits outside the training distribution in subtle, hard-to-detect ways. I designed and built a neural backend model to address this — implemented end-to-end in PyTorch, benchmarked across 5+ evaluation configurations — but more importantly, it taught me to question whether any metric is actually measuring what it claims to.

This instinct for construct validity, distribution shift, and failure mode analysis, paired with hands-on engineering of reproducible ML pipelines, is what I now want to bring to frontier AI systems as a research engineer — particularly agentic evaluation, where the gap between benchmark performance and real-world reliability is widening fast.
